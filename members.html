Ricardo Duran

Former CIS Club President

1) When did you graduate and what degree/certificate did you gain from it? I graduated Spring of 2017 with my AS in Network Administration & Security Management.

2) What's your current profession? I worked for the last 3.5 years as an IT Analyst for a Major Credit Bureau. I was working heavily with Cloud Technologies like AWS. I was very recently hired on to a public energy utility to work as an IT Associate rotating within 3 departments. I an currently working with the Networking and Architecture department. In this department I will be tasked with maintaining network data circuits and network appliances such as switches, routers, firewalls.

3) How did the CIS department/program help prepare you for your profession or in general? The Mt. SAC CIS department was key to preparing me for the IT jobs out there. Of course it is imperative that you learn new skills and technologies on your own by completing certificates or pursuing a higher education but the CIS department introduced me to people in IT with varying levels of experience. It was meeting those people that helped me get to where I am today.

4) What advice would you give for current students looking for jobs/internships? Study hard and NETWORK! You can be an nerd all you want but in the IT world you need people skills as much as you need technical skills; maybe even more so. I used to think that being the most knowledgeable person in the room was key to getting the job but I learned after countless interviews that I needed to know how to interact with people. Attend social mixers hosted by the college, career fairs, talk to your peers; these are all great ways to find your next path in IT.

5) Anything else you want to say? One LifeProTip I would offer you is to take care of your physical and mental health. IT is fun, challenging and full of free or subsidized food. Seriously, if you neglect your well-being you will gain TONS of weight and lose days of sleep. Have a good exercise routine and sleep schedule.
